**The Rituals & Ceremonies row (04) is COMPLETE** (this wave). NN/SS/EE/WW = 100; **A01-CC-04 (Central) ships SHORT at 43** (MP 25 / Chhattisgarh 18). Rituals are the **most web-tier-dominated** sub-concept (SANSKRITI's ritual pool is the noisiest — mixes festivals/dances/state ceremonies/culture-statements; Stage-2 yielded only ~1–5 clean anchors/cell) and the **hardest-gated** (the base model binds famous rites strongly but drops obscure tribal rites, so EE/WW/CC needed multiple confirm-and-emit research rounds to clear 100). Central (2 states: MP + Chhattisgarh) is short because Chhattisgarh's Bastar/Gond rites are mostly too obscure to pass the base-Llama ΔL gate; MP carries the cell via well-known Ujjain/Narmada/temple rites. CC-04 (43) beats CC-03 (30) and CC-02 (41 → ties). Two construct hardenings landed this row: (1) the **corruptor bank** was expanded 27→42 with long (6–8 tok) cross-region rituals (ritual names tokenize long — without it 27 South anchors were dropped at Stage-3 length-matching); (2) the **verify profile** was corrected so a rite of a religious/ethnic community whose homeland is the target state PASSES (**Sikh→Punjab**, Buddhist-monastic→Ladakh/Sikkim, tribal→state) — *pan-Sikh ≠ pan-Indian* — which lifted NN-04's Punjab 4→15 and the cell 96→100.

Eligibility expands — NN +Himachal Pradesh/J&K, SS +Puducherry/Lakshadweep, EE +Jharkhand/Arunachal, CC's Chhattisgarh now native. The 10 Festival/Costume cells were built under the old ≤3 cap; **Cuisine (03) onward use ≤5** (so NN-03 includes J&K & Himachal, SS-03 includes Puducherry).
**The Costume & Textile row (02) is COMPLETE.** A01-WW-02 (West) and A01-CC-02 (Central) ship as documented **short cells** at their true textile ceilings — West **64** (3 eligible states), Central **41** (2 eligible states).
